III -Wooden Fingers

I know your eyes, Monsieur. I know the gazeThat burns my fingers as they grazeThe battered silver of the varnished caneEyes like the whisper of a flame

My head is turned in mock disdainBut it is yet to see you stainedI glide my hand and eyes become a hazeIt is your blood that fills the wooden veins.

Come now, Monsieur, and lift your eyesTo mineI know desire and it can but rise
